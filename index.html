Internship

Upgrade of control programme for the POSEIDON silicon irradiation facility

My internship started the 27th of February 2023 and ended the 26th of May 2023. Before diving into the results of my internship, a short introduction about SCK CEN is in order. SCK CEN is a company located in Mol that researches nuclear waste and is a global leader in nuclear research, services and education.

SCK CEN has an ICT-division that aims to improve work inside as well as outside the company. As thousands of documents are present in this company that need to be stored, Alexandria was developed which is a site that can hold all these documents. It is due to people having trouble with finding and downloading these documents, an application was created to streamline this process by letting the user choose a directory and easily provide them with all the documents that accompany this directory. This is called Critical Documents Synchronisation Tool or CDST.

CDST had a few issues. Some of these were:

  • Documents get copied instead of downloaded
  • Tool crashes with weird character in the name
  • Errors were unclear
  • Stopping of automatic download happens instantly

These issues were the topic of my internship as it was my job to try and fix these.

This project consisted of multiple phases, the first of which was checking the old code. After this, small bugs, such as changing the downloaded documentâ€™s name, were fixed. Then, I changed the old code so that the documents get downloaded instead of copied. Finally, I added some quality of life items such as alerting the user when an error occurred.

On Wednesday the 20th of April 2022 I gave a training regarding smart-phones and social media to 8 elderly people for Professional Skills

LDC Luysterbos is a service center in Geel that helps elderly people and people who have financial issues.

To start I asked one of my colleagues to send a mail to our contact (who was Irmgard Schelles) for information regarding a training session that they asked for.

My colleague James sent a mail to our contact, and she responded saying that it's good that we wanted to do this training session/info session and that we have to contact her colleague Ellen Lievens.

I sent a mail to Ellen, asking her what date and time she wanted us to do the info session and if we could meet somewhere in advance to see what they expect from us.

She replied that she needed data so that she can publish when we were going to organize said session. She also said that on the 24th of May, around 13.30, she could meet us in advance.

The 24th of May rolled around, and we talked with Ellen about when we/they wanted the session to be. She said that we needed to send her information when we are available.

Later in the day I sent a mail to her saying that we could do it from the 4th of April until the 17th and only on Mondays (because none of us had a class then).

She said that it couldn't work during Easter, but that it can work on the 20th of April. She asked if that was OK with us. We said that it was fine.

After this, we talked about facebook and asked if our participants had any problems or questions regarding this topic.

Thereafter, we talked about smartphones and asked if anyone had any problems or questions.

We ended our presentation with some terminology of smartphones. And after that, we asked them to fill in some paperwork.


Conclusion

I have learned that elderly people are smarter than I expected.

Giving an info session was pretty fun, and I want to do it again.

Project 4.0 explanation

This is a group-project where all of IT-Factory come together to create something for a client.

This project checks if the student has the skill of working in a group to effectively give a good solution to the customer.

There are 2 phases to each project: ideation and realization.


Ideation phase

During this phase, each group has to analyse the project (Who, what and why).

So, a group has to analyze who the project is for, what they need to create and why its necessary.

After a group analyzed those items, they will analyze how they will tackle the project.

In this phase, I was lead of my group and I made sure that the morale was good and the items analyzed would be correct.

Our client was 3-IT and they wanted an easier way to read data from a digital meter. They would like it if the user sees graphs depending on the consumption or production of energy.

My other job was, same as the rest of my teammates, to research something that would help us create a working project. Each team member needed to research whatever was in their field, which was AI for me.

I searched high and low for a good visualization software that could show the user some very informative and easy to understand graphs.


Realization phase

In this phase, we needed to build the site.

I first tried working with Qlik to make good visualizations, but I couldn't move those visualizations to the site automatically without paying for it.

Because Qlik didn't work, I tried to use PowerBi. Sadly enough, this ended up with the same result as Qlik.

Tension in the team was getting higher, so I communicated with our leader to maybe use a javascript plugin. He agreed and I helped design the graphs.

In the end, we had a pretty good site with alot of information regarding consumption and production of energy. You can watch the video of our working project down below.

Data science explained

Data science is a combination of maths and statistics, specialized programming, advanced analytics, AI and machine learning.

Data science is the science of uncovering actionable insights hidden in an organization's data.

These insights can be used to guide decision making and strategic planning.

There are many programs that you can use to work on data science, but we used something called R.

Big Data Categorizing tanks

Categorizing tanks

What is big data

Big data is data that contains greater variety, arriving in increased volume and with more velocity.

This basically means creating enormous data sets.

Big data was a group project. Every member had the same responsibilities.


Categorizing tanks

The reason we chose different types of tanks was because of all the different wars involving them.

The original plan was to detect an enemy tank, but this caused problems. The biggest problem was that it was near impossible to check which tank was the enemy (it could be that a Russian tank has a different faction controlling it).

In the end we wanted to see if we could create a program that detects what tank is shown.

The first step was gathering images of tanks. We did this by using: frames of videos and an image scraper.

Next, we needed to clean the data. This means that we only need images of tanks, so images of a tank that is far away, but a shrubbery is near, needs to be cleaned so that the tank is more clearly visible.

After all the cleaning was done, we started training our model using the images.

When the training was done, we built a Streamlit application to visualize detecting the tank category.

Finally, we tested our model against Google Teachable Machine to see if we did it better, and the heavy weight model did.
